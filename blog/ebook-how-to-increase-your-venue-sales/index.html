New revenue opportunities for venue sales professionals

The events industry has shifted, and kept shifting. The virtual event market alone hit $114.2 billion in 2021, and the venues finding new ground are the ones willing to rethink how they sell, engage, and operate.

This free ebook is built for sales professionals who want practical strategies, not theory. Three areas, each with tools you can put to work straight away.

1. Build event engagement that converts

49% of marketers say audience engagement is the single biggest factor in event success. Passive attendance doesn't move the needle anymore. The ebook covers:

  • Social media as a year-round sales channel — how to audit what's worked, build a content mix that resonates, and use live Q&As, polls, and feedback loops to keep your audience warm between events

  • Building a 365-day event community — a step-by-step approach to creating a digital-first community that grows your brand organically and pulls more participants toward your venue

  • A template for immersive event design — covering the five elements that drive deep connection, active participation, inclusivity, and genuine impact

2. Find the revenue your venue is leaving on the table

Most venues are sitting on data they're not fully using. The ebook shows you how to turn it into income:

  • Data analytics in practice — customer segmentation for targeted marketing, dynamic pricing, sponsorship optimisation, and real-time adjustments based on engagement data

  • Automation that pays for itself — reduce admin costs, personalise interactions at scale, and use the key reports in the ebook to maintain cash flow through low-demand periods

  • Smarter booking engines — 24/7 availability, live pricing, no double-bookings, and behavioural data that helps you sell more without adding headcount.

3. Rethink the attendee experience

The details people remember aren't always the main event. Two areas are worth particular attention:

  • QR codes beyond check-in — spa packages, local tours, on-site add-ons guests can browse and book from their phones, driving revenue you'd otherwise miss

  • Hybrid events done properly — a practical checklist for running events that work for both in-room and remote audiences, with virtual networking built in rather than bolted on.
